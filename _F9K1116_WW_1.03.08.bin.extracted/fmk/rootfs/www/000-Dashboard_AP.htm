We think there's a wiring problem.

There should be a cable connecting your modem to the yellow port on your router. Your modem should be on.

There are a few things you can do to make sure things are ready to go:

  1. Make sure your modem is turned on. Its power adapter should be plugged in at both ends, and one or more lights on the modem should be on.
  2. There should be a network cable connecting your modem to the yellow port on your router. This cable carries the signal from your modem to your router; without it, you can't get online.
  3. If these are both as they should be, you might try restarting your modem by unplugging its power supply, waiting fifteen seconds, then plugging it back in. If your modem has a battery you may need to find the modem's reset button and use that instead.

Wiring

The most common wiring difficulty is between the modem and the router. It's important to check both ends of the cable. One end should be plugged into the yellow port on your new router; the other end should be connected to a similar port on your modem. Different modem makers label the ports differently; you might find it labeled "data", "LAN", "network", or "Ethernet".

We're having trouble talking with your Internet Service Provider.

Please restart your modem.

We're not getting an Internet connection, but we can talk to your modem just fine. So let's try restarting it.

  1. Turn off your modem. Usually this involves throwing a switch on the modem or simply unplugging its power supply. Some modems have a battery, in which case you'll need to find the modem's reset button.
  2. Wait 15 seconds.
  3. Turn your modem back on.

After several seconds its lights will be on and it'll be ready to talk to the router.

If this problem recurs, please contact your ISP to troubleshoot your account.

We'll watch for your modem to turn off and on, then we'll continue automatically.



Why restart the modem?

Some modems need to be restarted before they will talk to a new router or an unfamiliar computer. And if there's a hiccup at your Internet Service Provider, the modem can drop your connection unexpectedly. We can see your modem but not the Internet, so giving it a quick restart will most likely do the trick.
